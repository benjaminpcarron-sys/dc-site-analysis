## 3. Red Flags

### Material Concerns

1. **No Anchor Tenant / Off-taker Identified**
   - The prospectus markets the site speculatively. A 274 MW data center without a pre-committed tenant represents significant development risk. The JLL/Main Place listing language ("Data Center, Advanced Manufacturing, EV Company's Welcome") suggests they are still seeking a user.

2. **Generation Capacity Not Studied**
   - The Boxcar Study explicitly states: "This is not a resource capacity study and does not evaluate the availability of generation capacity." The transmission system can handle the load, but the availability of 274 MW of generation from MISO is not confirmed. In a tightening MISO capacity market, this is a material risk.

3. **Aggressive Ramp Timeline**
   - The ramp schedule shows 50 MW by 2026 and full 274 MW by January 2028. Given the Boxcar Study is dated April 2025 and the System Extension Guarantee Agreement (SEGA) has not yet been executed, delivering 50 MW by 2026 appears unrealistic. A more conservative estimate would be 2027 for first power and 2029 for full build.

4. **Justice40 Disadvantaged Community Not Disclosed**
   - The site is in a federally designated disadvantaged community with high diesel PM (82nd pct) and PM2.5 (85th pct) exposure. None of the prospectus documents mention this. Community engagement and potential opposition should be factored into the timeline.

5. **Site Acreage Inconsistency**
   - Documents cite 35 acres, 140 acres, and 147 acres depending on the document and context. The 35 AC appears to be a subset (one development pad); the 140-147 AC is the full parcel assemblage. A current survey is needed to confirm.

6. **CEII Designation**
   - The Boxcar Study is marked "Contains Critical Energy Infrastructure Information (CEII) — Do Not Release." This limits the ability to share the study's findings with potential investors or partners without Ameren's consent.

## 6. Summary Assessment

**Decatur Commerce Park is a real site with legitimate infrastructure** — the Ameren Boxcar Study confirms 274 MW of transmission capacity, the 138 kV interconnection is feasible at $7-9M, and the site is industrially zoned by-right. The Central Illinois Grid Transformation Program adds long-term grid resilience with multiple 345 kV upgrades planned within 150 km.

**However, this is speculative development with no anchor tenant, no disclosed rate, and an aggressive timeline.** The generation capacity question is unresolved, the site is in a Justice40 community (not disclosed), and the former Bridgestone-Firestone site needs environmental clearance. The timeline showing 50 MW by 2026 is not realistic given the SEGA has not been executed.

**Bottom line: Credible infrastructure play, but the investment case depends entirely on securing a tenant at an acceptable rate. The grid is there; the customer is not.**

### Ameren Illinois Large Load Framework

**Interconnection Thresholds:**
- Large data center defined as **50+ MW** in Ameren Illinois territory
- 50+ MW facilities interconnect at **transmission or subtransmission voltage** (not distribution)
- Minimal or no impact on distribution system for loads at this threshold

**Interconnection Process:**
- Managed through **MISO planning process** (not Ameren's internal grid plan)
- Transmission planning studies required to determine system upgrade needs
- Ameren requires **early developer engagement** and tracks requests as "step changes" in peak demand forecasts

**Relevant ICC Dockets:**
1. **[Docket 25-0083](https://icc.illinois.gov/docket/P2025-0083/documents/369035)** — Revenue-neutral rate design (Section 16-105.5 PUA). MYRP tariffs effective Dec 26, 2023. Evidentiary hearing May 13, 2025; Final Order Aug 7, 2025.
2. **[Docket 26-0051](https://icc.illinois.gov/docket/P2026-0051/documents/375149)** — Ameren Multi-Year Integrated Grid Plan (2028-2031). Pages 156-157 specifically address large load/data center interconnection requirements.

**Implications for Commerce Park 80 (274 MW):**
- At 274 MW, this project is well above the 50 MW threshold -- transmission-level interconnection is confirmed (consistent with the Boxcar Study's 138 kV POI)
- The MISO planning process governs upgrades, which adds timeline risk vs. a utility-controlled process
- No published large load tariff -- Ameren IL uses bilateral agreements and the MISO process rather than a formal tariff schedule like AEP Ohio or Dominion
- The 2028-2031 Grid Plan (Docket 26-0051) should be reviewed for any Macon County-specific transmission investments

### Supply-Demand Balance & Capacity Outlook (2027-2030)

**Source:** Halcyon Query across 459 ICC documents matching "Ameren Illinois" + "capacity" + "load forecast" (7 documents cited)

**Critical: Ameren Illinois does not own or operate generation.** Resource adequacy is managed by MISO and the Illinois Power Agency (IPA), not Ameren. This means power supply for a 274 MW data center depends entirely on MISO market availability, not Ameren's portfolio.

**Peak Load Forecasts:**
- Ameren IL provides 5-year load forecasts to the IPA annually (high/low/expected scenarios)
- Peak load **decreased 15%** between 2012-2023, while customer count grew 1%
- Under high switching scenarios, 80% residential and 99% small commercial switching to alternative suppliers projected

**Generation Capacity:**
- Capacity outlook increasingly relies on DERs (solar + storage, demand response)
- Solar and wind interconnections growing meaningfully as of Oct 2025
- DERs expected to shift/reduce load to defer new supply-side capacity

**MISO Zone 4 (Ameren Illinois) Capacity Concerns:**
- IPA has expressed concern about MISO Planning Resource Auction (PRA) price volatility
- Small supply deficits can lead to significantly higher prices (vertical demand curve)
- Coal plant retirements and changing MISO/FERC rules create uncertainty
- Ameren IL's residential demand response: only 15.9 MW cleared in 2020 PRA

**Climate and Reliable Grid Affordability Act (CRGA):**
- Enacted January 2026 -- new statewide planning requirements for reliability during clean energy transition
- 2025 statewide resource adequacy study evaluated long-term capacity conditions and retirement risks

**Implications for Commerce Park 80 (274 MW):**
- **Generation procurement is the #1 risk.** Ameren can't supply generation -- it must come from MISO market or bilateral PPAs. The Boxcar Study's explicit disclaimer confirms this.
- Adding 274 MW of load in a territory where peak demand has been *declining* represents a massive step change. This will likely require new generation to be built or contracted.
- MISO Zone 4 capacity auction volatility means energy costs are unpredictable
- The CRGA may create new planning requirements that could benefit or complicate large load interconnections

### MISO Zone 4 Resource Adequacy Study Findings

**Source:** Halcyon Query across ICC documents, 2025 date filter (3 documents cited)

**The 2025 Draft REAP identifies the MISO Zone 4 resource adequacy gap as urgent.** Illinois may require new capacity procurement mechanisms.

**Key findings:**

- **NERC LTRA (2023):** MISO projected to have **insufficient resources region-wide starting in the 2028-2029 delivery year** to meet load and target reserve margins
- **2024-2025 delivery year:** MISO PRA showed sufficient capacity in Zone 4 (central + southern IL)
- **2025-2026 delivery year:** MISO-OMS survey projects capacity could range from a **1.1 GW surplus to a 2.7 GW deficit**, depending on pace of new resource additions
- **2022-2023 capacity auction:** Zone 4 experienced a capacity shortfall -- prices reached **$236.66/MW-day**. Zone 4 **imported over 2,300 MW of its nearly 10,000 MW need** (23% import dependency)
- **CEJA retirements:** ICC supporting RTO reforms to plan for fossil fuel retirements. Proactive transmission planning being assessed to mitigate emissions leakage

**This means:** Commerce Park 80's proposed January 2028 energization coincides exactly with when NERC projects MISO will have **insufficient resources**. A 274 MW new load in an already import-dependent zone during a capacity shortfall period represents significant generation procurement risk.
