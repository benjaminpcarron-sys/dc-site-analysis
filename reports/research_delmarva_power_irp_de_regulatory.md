## Key Findings

- **Interconnection Pause in Effect:** Delaware PSC Docket No. 25-0826 has **paused all new large-load (≥25 MW) interconnections** in Delmarva Power territory until a large-load tariff is established. This directly affects any 500 MW data center proposal at the Millsboro site.
- **Large-Load Tariff Under Development:** Delmarva filed a proposed tariff Dec 19, 2025. Written comments closed April 6, 2026. **Delmarva must file final tariff by April 27, 2026.** Hearing examiner findings expected summer 2026; full commission deliberation follows.
- **~2.5 GW of DC Demand in Pipeline:** Delmarva is working with 5 data center developers totaling ~2,455 MW (nearly doubling Delaware's 2.7 GW summer peak). The largest is Project Washington (1,200 MW) in New Castle County.
- **No Recent IRP on File:** The last public Delmarva Power IRP was filed **November 30, 2016** (PSC Docket). DE regulations require IRP filings every 2 years, but the requirement has been relaxed — Delmarva only files when changing supply sources or directed by PSC. **No 2024-2026 IRP is publicly available.**
- **All Known DC Projects Are in New Castle County:** Project Washington (1.2 GW), Frightland/Middletown (600-1,000 MW), Newark (130-250 MW). **No publicly disclosed data center projects in Sussex County.** The Millsboro site would be a greenfield entrant in an area with no existing DC cluster.
- **PJM RTEP Driving Eastern Upgrades:** PJM's 2025 RTEP Window 1 includes ~$11.6B in projects, with the Delmarva zone specifically called out for needs driven by load growth + offshore wind delays. Details on Delmarva-specific projects not yet published.

## Regulatory Proceedings

### 1. PSC Docket No. 25-0826 — Large Load Tariff
- **Filed:** September 3, 2025 (joint petition by PSC Staff & Division of Public Advocate)
- **Commission:** Delaware Public Service Commission
- **What it does:**
  - Opens proceeding to develop large-load tariff (≥25 MW customers)
  - **Pauses interconnection** of all new large-load facilities in Delmarva territory
  - Delmarva supported both measures
- **Tariff Structure (Proposed):**
  - Applies to customers with peak demand ≥25 MW (Delmarva recommends raising to 50 MW)
  - Defines qualification criteria, upfront financial commitments, grid upgrade cost assignment, and contract terms for scaling/withdrawal
  - Central issue: preventing infrastructure cost burden from landing on residential/small-business customers
- **Key Timeline:**
  - Written comments deadline: April 6, 2026 (extended from March 6)
  - **Delmarva final tariff filing: April 27, 2026**
  - Hearing examiner findings: Summer 2026
  - Full commission deliberation: Late 2026
- **Parties:** Delmarva Power, PSC Staff, Division of Public Advocate, DNREC, Starwood Digital Ventures (granted intervention), union representatives
- **Source:** [DE PSC Announcement](https://depsc.delaware.gov/2025/10/14/delaware-psc-opens-docket-for-large-load-tariff-pauses-interconnections/)

### 2. PSC Docket No. 25-1555 — Delmarva Power Rate Case
- **Filed:** December 9, 2025
- **Commission:** Delaware PSC
- **Request:** $67.8M increase in annual distribution revenue (21.7% increase over existing distribution base rates)
- **Relevance:** Sets the baseline distribution rate that any large-load tariff would layer on top of
- **Source:** [DE State News](https://news.delaware.gov/2026/01/12/delmarva-power-files-request-for-67-8-million-base-rate-increase/)

### 3. Delmarva Power IRP (Most Recent: 2016)
- **Filed:** November 30, 2016
- **Commission:** Delaware PSC
- **Regulatory basis:** 26 Del. C. §1007(c)(1) and 26 Del. Admin. C. §3010
- **Status:** No subsequent public IRP filing identified. DE regulations have relaxed the biennial filing requirement — Delmarva now files only when electing to change supply sources or as directed by PSC.
- **Gap:** The 2016 IRP predates the current data center demand wave entirely. There is no public load forecast from Delmarva that incorporates the ~2.5 GW DC pipeline.
- **Source:** [DE PSC Electric Regulation](https://depsc.delaware.gov/electric-regulation/)

## Implications for Millsboro Site (499 Mitchell St)

### Critical Risk: Interconnection Pause
The PSC's pause on large-load interconnections (Docket 25-0826) **blocks any new ≥25 MW facility from connecting** until the tariff is finalized. At the current pace, the pause likely lifts late 2026 at the earliest. A 500 MW facility at this site cannot begin interconnection proceedings until then.

### Tariff Uncertainty
The large-load tariff structure is still being defined. Key unknowns:
- Final MW threshold (25 MW vs. 50 MW)
- Grid upgrade cost allocation (developer-funded vs. socialized)
- Financial commitment/security requirements
- Penalty structure for project scaling back or default

### No IRP Load Forecast
Without a current IRP, there is no public Delmarva load forecast that accounts for DC demand. This makes it harder to assess how the utility plans to serve incremental load on the Delmarva Peninsula, particularly in Sussex County where grid capacity is already limited (~200 MW estimated without upgrades).

### Greenfield Advantage/Risk
Being the only known project in Sussex County is a double-edged sword:
- **Advantage:** No queue congestion from competing DC projects in the area
- **Risk:** No existing infrastructure buildout momentum; all transmission upgrades must be justified by this single project

### PJM Eastern Corridor
The broader PJM RTEP investment in eastern transmission ($11.6B) could eventually improve deliverability to the Delmarva Peninsula, but these are multi-year projects unlikely to be in service before 2030+.
