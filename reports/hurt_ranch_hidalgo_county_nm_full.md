## Halcyon Regulatory Research — SPP / Tri-State

### SPP Large Load Framework (from 193 SPP documents)

**HILL (High Impact Large Load) Processes:**
- SPP Revision Requests **RR 696** and **RR 634** establish HILL processes
- SPP proposed **"CHILLS" (Conditional High Impact Large Load Service)** — allows large loads to be served via non-firm conditional transmission service
- This is directly relevant to the Columbus Electric interconnection path

**SPP RTO Western Expansion (RR 594):**
- Targeted for **April 2026** implementation
- FERC accepted revisions March 20, 2025 with conditions
- SPP must notify FERC of actual effective date 6 months prior
- **This is the Tri-State migration referenced in the Questions for Ian doc**

**SPP Demand Response for Large Loads (RR 703):**
- Addresses "rapid load growth and tightening energy constraints"
- Full implementation: **2028 summer season**
- Transitional 2027 implementation with caps
- Controllable non-registered DR capped at 2,152 MW
- Peak Demand Assessment holds Load Responsible Entities accountable

### Key Implications for Site Layer 1
1. **Columbus is NOT joining SPP** (per Questions for Ian doc) — stays under Tri-State rules
2. **HILT was rejected** at FERC in October 2025 and not refiled — fallback framework unclear
3. **BYOR (Bring Your Own Resources)** submitted to Columbus at 715 MW but contingent on HILT
4. **45 MW ESA** with Columbus pending — this is the first step
5. **115 kV is limiting** — noted in portfolio review as "small for 2 GW of solar"
6. **Southline 345 kV project** (43 km away) could be the long-term grid solution

## Risk Assessment

|---|---|---|
| Grid insufficient (115 kV for 214+ MW) | Major upgrades needed; Southline 345 kV is 43 km away | 50% |
| Columbus not joining SPP | Interconnection path unclear; no HILT, no SPP queue | 40% |
| BYOR contingent on HILT (rejected) | 715 MW generation plan without regulatory framework | 35% |
| Remote location (no fiber, water, gas, highways) | All infrastructure must be built from scratch | 30% |
| Justice40 community | EJ engagement required; potential opposition | 10% |
| Seismic SDC C | Minor cost impact (~5%) | 5% |

**Overall development risk: HIGH** — The interconnection pathway is the critical uncertainty. Without HILT or SPP queue access, Columbus Electric's legacy Tri-State framework may not support a 214 MW load interconnection.
