### Query 1: SPP Large Load Framework (193 SPP documents)

**HILL (High Impact Large Load) Processes:**
- SPP Revision Requests **RR 696** and **RR 634** establish HILL framework
- SPP proposed **"CHILLS" (Conditional High Impact Large Load Service)** — non-firm conditional transmission service for large loads
- **HILL was approved by FERC January 15, 2026** — provides 90-day study-and-approval process

**SPP RTO Western Expansion (RR 594):**
- Targeted for **April 2026** implementation
- FERC accepted revisions March 20, 2025 (Docket ER24-2184)
- Tri-State transitioning from WACM to SPP West BAA
- **Columbus Electric is NOT joining SPP** — stays under Tri-State legacy rules

**SPP Demand Response (RR 703):**
- Addresses "rapid load growth and tightening energy constraints"
- Full implementation: 2028 summer season
- Controllable non-registered DR capped at 2,152 MW

### Query 2: Tri-State Resource Adequacy (419 SPP documents)

**Tri-State's position in SPP West BAA:**

- LOLE modeling includes external non-firm assistance from WACM and WECC BAs across SPP DC ties
- **Potential supply shortfalls during loss-of-load hours** — studies indicate capacity "likely available" from external entities

**Resource adequacy concerns:**
- Cost shift risks being addressed through SPP stakeholder process
- Integration risks in CRSP and LAP zones — withdrawal of major transmission owner would render membership untenable for others
- No specific capacity breakdown by fuel type available in public filings

## 6. Red Flags

1. **Columbus not joining SPP** — Regulatory orphan risk. HILL/CHILLS don't apply to Columbus under Tri-State legacy rules.
2. **HILT rejected, BYOR contingent** — 715 MW BYOR depends on a rejected framework. SPP HILL is a different program.
3. **115 kV grid radically insufficient** — 214 MW on 69/115 kV serving a co-op with ~25 MW peak is a 10x step change.
4. **Extreme remoteness** — No fiber, water, gas, or highways within 30 km. $50-100M+ in non-power infrastructure.
5. **Tri-State resource adequacy relies on imports** — 110 MW Stegall DC tie and non-firm external assistance.
6. **No NM data center tax incentives** in our database.

## Summary Assessment

**Hurt Ranch is a long-horizon land play, not a near-term DC development.** The 214 MW target is 10x the serving co-op's peak load, on a 69/115 kV system with no supporting infrastructure within 30 km. The interconnection pathway is unclear — Columbus isn't joining SPP, HILT was rejected, and BYOR is in limbo.

**The Southline 345 kV project (43 km, Phase 1 by 2028) is the key catalyst.** If Southline delivers on schedule, it transforms the grid picture. The site's value proposition is: cheap land + abundant solar resource + proximity to future 345 kV backbone.

**Near-term path to value:** Secure the 45 MW ESA with Columbus, build Phase 1 as a smaller behind-the-meter solar+storage facility, and wait for Southline to enable the full 214+ MW vision.
